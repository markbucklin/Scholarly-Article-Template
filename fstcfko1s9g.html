Assessing stem cell function by continuous measurement of network activity

Characteristics of spontaneous activity change progressively during cortical development. Initially, the network activity is dominated by highly recurrent pattern of synchronous network activity, referred to as "network bursts". This short burst is 0.5 - 2 seconds in duration and 0.1 - 0.5 Hz in firing rate and is followed by phases of a silent network recovery and a low level activity before the next initiation.
