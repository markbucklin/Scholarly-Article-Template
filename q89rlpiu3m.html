Technical advances in the tools available to visualize and manipulate dynamic cellular processes in real time have provided an unprecedented lens to probe the complex neural circuits in vivo. Using a wide-field fluorescence microscope with a scientific-CMOS camera, we can record activity in hundreds of distinct neurons across wide areas of the brain of awake behaving mice9. Additionally, when combined with pharmacological manipulation and/or electrophysiological recording, optical imaging provides a sophisticated approach to investigate neuronal function within the larger neural network (ref).
