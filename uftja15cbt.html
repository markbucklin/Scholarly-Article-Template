Glass cranial windows have been widely adopted for in vivo optical imaging because they permit long-term and high-resolution observation of individual neurons, neural networks or vasculature in various cortical regions. The general approach of the method is to seal a cover glass over an exposed brain region (after craniotomy), to prevent infection and to maintain the physiological condition of the tissue. However, once the cover glass is installed, it is difficult to access the brain underlying the window to inject virus or to deliver drugs or chemicals for any cellular manipulation.
