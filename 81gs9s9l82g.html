Imaging Calcium dynamics in behaving mouse

Using a conventional epifluorescence microscopy techniques with a scientific-CMOS (sCMOS) camera, we are able to record neural activity from hundreds to thousands of neurons at single-cell resolution. Increasing fraction of observable neurons may provide better insights into neural coding, or may lead a better understanding of neural function of transplanted cells within larger multicellular networks. Yet such experiments have not been pursued thus far.
